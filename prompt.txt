You are a cricket analytics expert. I have uploaded a CSV file containing IPL 2026 player data with batting and bowling statistics.

Your task is to add a new column called `player_category` to this CSV and assign each player to one of three categories: **A**, **B**, or **C**.

## Classification Rules

Use the following performance-based criteria to categorize players. The goal is a **nearly equal distribution** across A, B, and C (aim for roughly 33% in each).

### Category A — Elite Performers
Players who are top contributors with strong match impact. Criteria (meet ANY of these):
- Batting average (bat_average) ≥ 30 AND strike rate (bat_sr) ≥ 130
- Bowling average (bowl_avg) ≤ 22 AND economy (bowl_eco) ≤ 8.0
- 3+ centuries (bat_100s ≥ 3) OR 50+ wickets (bowl_wickets ≥ 50)
- All-rounders: bat_runs ≥ 1000 AND bowl_wickets ≥ 30

### Category B — Solid Contributors
Players who are reliable and consistent but not elite. Criteria (meet ANY of these after excluding Category A):
- bat_average between 20 and 30 OR bat_runs ≥ 500
- bowl_wickets between 15 and 49 OR bowl_avg between 22 and 30
- bat_matches ≥ 30 (experienced players with decent records)

### Category C — Emerging / Limited Data
Players who do not qualify for A or B. Includes:
- Newcomers with few matches or limited stats
- Players with below-average performance metrics
- Players with mostly missing/blank statistics

## Important Instructions

1. **Handle missing values**: If a stat field is blank or missing, do NOT penalize the player — treat it as insufficient data and lean toward Category C unless other stats clearly indicate A or B.

2. **Balance the distribution**: After initial classification, if the distribution is imbalanced (e.g., too many C players), adjust thresholds slightly to achieve approximately equal distribution across all three categories. The final output should have roughly 30-36% of players in each category.

3. **Consider all-rounders fairly**: Players who contribute with both bat and ball should be evaluated on their combined contribution.

4. **Output format**: Return the complete CSV with the original columns intact and the new `player_category` column appended as the last column. Provide the CSV as a downloadable file.

5. **Summary table**: After providing the CSV, also give me a summary showing:
   - Count and percentage of players in each category
   - Top 3 players in each category with their key stats
   - A brief justification for borderline cases
